Case study · 2014—2024

Mount Vernon Website Redesign

RoleVP, Media & Communications
OrganizationGeorge Washington’s Mount Vernon

The brief

Reposition Mount Vernon’s digital footprint from a brochure-style site for ticket buyers into the leading educational resource on George Washington — and turn that into a sustainable engine for visitation, e-commerce, membership, and earned revenue.

What we did

  • Rebuilt the site around long-form, SEO-led content marketing, pushing dozens of Washington-era topics into the top 2–3 organic search positions.
  • Migrated web and database platforms to AWS, cutting operating costs ~35% while improving reliability.
  • Replatformed e-commerce on a custom stack supporting tickets, donations, memberships, and donor retention together.
  • Layered in segmented email, mobile optimization, and a centralized CRM tied to ticketing and membership data.
8M+annual visitors (from 2.5M)
3.5mavg dwell time
$8.6Me-commerce (from $1.1M)
35%infra cost reduction

Why it mattered

For most Americans, the website is the first — and sometimes only — visit to Mount Vernon they’ll ever take. Treating it as the front door rather than the brochure changed the institution’s reach by an order of magnitude and turned digital into a self-funding part of the operating model.

Case study · 2017—2018 · Thea Award

Be Washington Interactive Theater

RoleDirector, end-to-end creation
Capital$3.5M, 36-seat theater

The brief

Build a permanent interpretive experience that puts visitors in George Washington’s seat — making impossible decisions, weighing real historical inputs, and feeling the weight of leadership in his own time.

What we did

  • Directed end-to-end creation of the $3.5M capital project — narrative, scripts, advisor recruitment, casting, set design, and the technology stack.
  • Each scenario is anchored in primary-source research, with on-screen advisors played by recognizable actors arguing each side in Washington’s ear.
  • Visitors vote on their decision in real time; the theater reveals the room’s consensus and Washington’s actual choice.
  • Built a companion online platform so the experience could be used in classrooms nationally.
TheaOutstanding Achievement Award (2018)
36-seatpermanent installation
$3.5Mcapital project

Why it mattered

It’s rare for a historic-site interpretive experience to win the same recognition as a major theme park attraction. Be Washington demonstrated that civic and historical content, given enough craft and risk, can compete with anything in the immersive entertainment world.

Case study · 2024—Present

TRPL AI Collections Platform

RoleProject Lead
OrganizationTheodore Roosevelt Presidential Library

The brief

Make the Theodore Roosevelt Presidential Library’s collection — letters, speeches, photographs, ephemera — explorable in plain language, on day one, by anyone with a question about TR.

What we did

  • Partnered with the collections and curatorial team to define what “safe” AI-assisted exploration of a presidential library’s holdings looks like.
  • Built a GPT-powered retrieval layer over the digitized collection so visitors and researchers can ask natural-language questions and get sourced, citable answers.
  • Designed the experience so that the AI always points back to the underlying primary source — not paraphrase, not hallucinate.

Why it mattered

Cultural institutions sit on enormous, under-indexed collections. Used carefully, conversational AI can lower the bar to research access from “know the finding aid” to “ask a question,” without sacrificing scholarly integrity. The Roosevelt Library will open with that capability on day one.

Personal project · 2026

Travel Passport

StackVanilla JS + D3 + Python
SourceTripIt + BTS + FAA

What it is

A virtual passport built from a decade of my TripIt flight history. Seven tabs: a flippable passport book with customs-style stamps for every country and U.S. state I’ve visited; a Stats tab with Flighty-style summary cards; a flat-and-globe World map with great-circle routes; a U.S. state choropleth; 130+ travel achievements; a timeline scrubber that plays back my travel month by month; and a full log of every flight.

How it works

  • OAuth-pulled flight history from TripIt is enriched with tail-number lookups against the BTS On-Time Performance dataset and the FAA Aircraft Registry — so each flight knows its specific aircraft and manufacture year.
  • Every map, stat, and achievement is computed client-side from a few JSON files; no backend.
  • A GitHub Actions workflow runs weekly, re-fetches my latest flights, re-runs the enrichments, and re-deploys to GitHub Pages automatically.

Personal project · 2026

Briney Family Genealogy AI

StackPython + LLM agents
SourcesWikiTree · FamilySearch · Find A Grave

What it is

An AI-driven research engine for the Briney family GEDCOM. It scans the tree for promising research targets — missing dates, zero-lifespan anomalies, date discrepancies, broken links, immigrant founders, military service, famous-cousin connections — then investigates each one across cached WikiTree data, web search, Find A Grave, FamilySearch, Wikipedia, and a corpus of published family-history books.

How it works

  • Skill-driven pipeline: an LLM agent prioritizes research targets, drafts findings, and prepares GEDCOM edits.
  • Every change goes through a “bio guard” protection step and a human-readable findings report before being applied.
  • The GEDCOM stays the single source of truth — the AI proposes; I approve.

Why I like it

Genealogy is one of those domains where the work isn’t writing — it’s checking. Letting an agent surface 100 candidate facts in an hour, with sources and confidence, turns a hobby that used to take years into something I can move forward on a Sunday afternoon.
